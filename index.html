This work is very much in progress. Stay tuned for results!

Prior Research

Detailed below, my doctoral dissertation centered on the genetic and immunological basis of species interactions, particularly rapid evolution of host resistance to macroparasites. I work across a range of biological scales, from field natural history and field experiments, to laboratory infections on whole organisms, to cell culture-based experiments.

Large scale gene expression

I use my own high-throughput, low-cost 3'RNAseq library construction method to ask how biotic and abiotic stresses alter gene expression

Trans-species epistasis

How do species interact on the level of gene expression? Hosts and parasites regulate not only their own gene expression, but also each other's. Who is really in control?

Genome editing with CRISPR/Cas9

Genetic mapping through QTL/eQTL and gene expression suggests candidate genes and regulatory loci that may underlie complex phenotypes. The next step is to mechanistically, rather than statistically, link genotype to phenotype. CRISPR/Cas9 in embryos and primary cell culture is the new gold standard for establishing causality
